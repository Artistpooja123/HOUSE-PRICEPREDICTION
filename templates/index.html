Under the hood

How the machine learning works

Three regression algorithms are trained and compared; the most accurate one is deployed automatically.

Collect & clean the dataset

1,200 property records are cleaned, missing values are handled, and categorical fields like location and condition are encoded.

Scale the features

Numeric features are standardized with StandardScaler so every algorithm sees data on a comparable scale.

Train three models

Linear Regression, Decision Tree, and Random Forest Regressors are trained on an 80/20 train-test split.

Compare & select

Each model is scored on MAE, MSE, RMSE and R². The best performer is saved as house_model.pkl.

Serve predictions live

Flask loads the saved model and scaler to turn your form input into an instant price estimate.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How accurate is the price prediction?+
The best-performing model reaches roughly 96% R² on held-out test data, but real-world prices depend on many factors this demo dataset can't fully capture.
What data does the model use?+
Area, bedrooms, bathrooms, floors, parking, year built, location, condition, and nearby schools, hospitals, and metro access.
Is my prediction history stored anywhere?+
History is saved only in your browser's LocalStorage — it never leaves your device.
Can I retrain the model on my own data?+
Yes — replace dataset/House_Price_Dataset.csv with your own CSV in the same format and re-run train_model.py.
